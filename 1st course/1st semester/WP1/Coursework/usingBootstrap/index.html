What are Liminal Spaces?

Definition

Liminal spaces are places of transition - hallways, empty parking lots, abandoned overpasses. They are "in-between" places where we don't linger, only pass through. When empty and devoid of people, they evoke a strange feeling.

Psychology

These spaces create a sense of déjà vu and nostalgia. They are simultaneously familiar and alien, real and dreamlike. Our brain expects movement and activity, but encounters emptiness and silence.

Aesthetics

Characterized by fluorescent lighting, monotonous colors, endless corridors and symmetrical structures. They look artificial, like scenery from a dream or video game.

Cultural Phenomenon

Interest in liminal spaces has grown online in recent years, spawned by "backrooms" and similar concepts. They symbolize modern isolation and the search for meaning.
